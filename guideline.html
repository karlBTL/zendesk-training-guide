üìù Communicating on the Ticket

Public Comment

  • This is the standard, outward-facing reply that **your customer will see**.
  • Use for all direct communication (answering questions, asking for details, confirming resolution).
  • **Used by:** CSRs and Light Agents (as a reader/collaborator).

Internal Note (Internal Comment)

  • This comment is **only visible to other agents** and internal team members. **The customer cannot see it.**
  • Use for: Handing off, sharing sensitive info, documenting internal actions.
  • **Used by:** CSRs and Light Agents.

**Light Agents are ONLY allowed to submit Internal Notes.** They can read Public Comments but cannot submit outward-facing replies to the customer.

‚è±Ô∏è Managing the Ticket Status

The status reflects where the ticket is in the workflow. Always ensure the status is accurate!

Status Meaning & Action
New The ticket has just been created and currently **has no assignee**. This is the starting status.
Open The ticket is actively being worked on by an agent or waiting for the next action *from an agent*.
Pending You are **waiting for a response or action from the customer**. Pauses the SLA clock.
On-Hold You are **waiting for a response or action from a third party or another internal team** (not the customer). Pauses the SLA clock.
Solved The customer's issue has been fully resolved and the agent has taken the final action.
Closed The ticket is permanently archived and **cannot be reopened or replied to**.

üè∑Ô∏è Fields, Priority, and Automation

Form & Subfields

  • **Form:** The initial classification (e.g., *Billing Inquiry*) that dictates the set of visible fields.
  • **Subfields:** Additional, granular fields (e.g., *Product Name*) that capture specific data for reporting.

Tags

  • Flexible **keywords/labels** (e.g., `refund_request`) added manually or automatically.
  • Used to group, filter, and report on tickets *after* initial classification.

Priority

  • Indicates the **severity or urgency** (e.g., *Low* to *Urgent*).
  • Guides agents on which tickets to address first to meet SLAs.

Assignee

  • The specific agent or group **currently responsible** for resolving the ticket.
  • A ticket should *always* have an assignee when actively worked on.

Macro

  • A **pre-recorded set of actions** applied with one click (inserting replies, setting status, tags, etc.).
  • Use for quick, consistent, and accurate responses to common questions.

Ticket Assignment & Handling

  • **Legal Cases:** Assign to **Laurent**. Rafi and Emmerance are included as Light Agents (internal review only).

Special Handling & Escalation

  • **Angry/Upset Customer:** The assigned **CSR** must first attempt to de-escalate the situation. If de-escalation fails, **escalate immediately to Management/Laurel**.

Mandatory CSR Tasks

  • **Suspended Tickets:** CSRs are responsible for regularly checking the **Suspended Tickets** queue and restoring/deleting tickets as needed.
  • **Language Handling:** **Always reply in the customer's language.** Use an internal AI translation tool (e.g., Google Translate, DeepL) to accurately understand and compose replies.
